Marriage and Divorce Study

This page includes plots that showcase historical marriage rates and divorce rates on both the national and state levels. All marriage and divorce rates are per 1,000 total population, and the data spans the years of 1990 to 2019.


Summary: Average Marriage Rate by State

This map displays the average state rate for each state. The top 5 are Nevada (50.3), Hawaii (18.3), Arkansas (11.9), Tennessee (10.6) and Idaho (9.8). In this dataset Nevada is a major outlier due to their marriage laws. The majority of the USA marriage rate ranges from 12-5. Hover over a state to identify their marriage rate.


Summary: Marriage Rates over Time by State

When plotted versus time, the general downward trend in marriages is apparent on the state-by-state level. Despite such a negative trend, there are some states for which there was an uptick between 2008 and 2014. This is a potential indication that there are event-driven effects that are captured in the marriage rate statistics, however deeper analysis is required to understand any correlations that may exist.

Summary: Marriage & Divorce Rates by State Bar Graph

The general trend for both marriage rates and divorce rates are on the decline, but the rates vary from state to state. The access to both state and national data allows comparisons to be made between states and to the national average. Analysis can lead to conclusions beyond the visualizations, and trends can be identified and characterized.

The behavior of marriage and divorce rates can be driven by historical events, such as political policy, natural disasters, and economic factors. In-depth analysis may provide correlations between such key events to effects on the marriage and divorce rates year-by-year and state-by-state.

The acquisition of specific data sets could illuminate factors that are driving the observed trends. Factors such as women's rights, birth control use, cohabitation, education level, and income levels could have impact on the marriage and divorce rates on the local and national levels. More targeted surveying can deepen the collective understanding of underlying forces that shape marriage and divorce decisions.

The collection and analysis of data can explain and predict the evolution of marriage and divorce in the United States.
