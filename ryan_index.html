The general trend for both marriage rates and divorce rates are on the decline, but the rates vary from state to state. The access to both state and national data allows comparisons to be made between states and to the national average. Analysis can lead to conclusions beyond the visualizations, and trends can be identified and characterized.

The behavior of marriage and divorce rates can be driven by historical events, such as political policy, natural disasters, and economic factors. In-depth analysis may provide correlations such key events to effects on the marriage and divorce rates year-by-year and state-by-state.

The acquisition of specific data sets could illuminate factors that are driving the observed trends. Factors such as women's rights, birth control use, cohabitation, education level, and income levels could have impact on the marriage and divorce rates on the local and national levels. More targeted surveying can deepen the collective understanding of underlying forces that shape marriage and divorce decisions.

The collection and analysis of data can explain and predict the evolution of marriage and divorce in the United States.
